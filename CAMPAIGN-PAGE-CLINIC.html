Story

In many rural communities across the Philippines, families often travel hours just to reach the nearest health center.
Simple illnesses go untreated, pregnant women lack proper prenatal check-ups, and children miss essential vaccinations because transportation is either too expensive or unavailable.
Over time, these small problems grow into life-threatening conditions that could have been prevented with timely medical care.
This campaign exists to change that reality for the people who need it most.

The Mobile Health Clinic will bring doctors, nurses, check-ups, vaccinations, and basic treatments directly to underserved barangays. Equipped with essential tools and staffed by compassionate medical professionals, this clinic will travel from community to community—making healthcare accessible, affordable, and consistent. Your support helps ensure that no family is left behind simply because the nearest clinic is too far away.

What is the campaign about?

This campaign aims to fund a mobile medical clinic that will deliver free or low-cost healthcare services to rural barangays. The goal is to provide check-ups, vaccinations, wound care, consultations, and health education to communities with limited access to hospitals.

What will you do with the funds? (₱20,000 Budget)

• ₱6,000 — Medical supplies (stethoscopes, BP monitors, thermometers, first-aid kits)
• ₱5,000 — Medicines (antibiotics, pain relievers, wound care essentials, vitamins)
• ₱4,000 — Fuel and transportation for clinic rounds
• ₱3,000 — Portable tables, chairs, and patient setup materials
• ₱2,000 — Health education materials and outreach information

Where will it be located?

The mobile clinic will operate across rural communities in Nueva Ecija, focusing on remote barangays where medical access is most limited.
